Kenya and Tanzania leading the pack in Internet Infrastructure


The Internet has witnessed a dramatic evolution since its launch in 1995, presenting a myriad of opportunities to society and to enterprises, in particular, through access to information in Kenya (Digital Kenya, World Bank). The most dramatic increase in internet happened in 2018, when the number of secure internet servers per 1 mil population grew 6 times year-on-year. The rest of East Africa are not far behind. Despite its relative underdevelopment compared to the league of East African community, Ethiopia is also catching up quick in the internet game.

Kenya Trailblazing


In terms of investment in network quality; however, Kenya and Uganda leads the pack. International Internet bandwidth available per Internet User increased more than 80% and 20% annually in the past 3 years, in Kenya and Uganda. Meanwhile, the others are not quite meeting the demands of users, which suggests significant growth potential for investment in network and connectivity infrastructure in Ethiopia, Rwanda and Tanzania.

Number of Secure Internet Server
per 1 million User

The number of distinct, publicly-trusted TLS/SSL certificates found in the Netcraft Secure Server Survey. The variable shows how Secure Internet servers (per 1 million people) varies by country.

International Internet Bandwidth (bit/s) per Internet user

The total traffic load of international fibre-optic cables and radio links for carrying Internet traffic in a year. The variable measures network quality in each country
