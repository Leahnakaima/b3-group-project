Growing Middle Class and Consumption Driving East Africa into Digital Technology and Services

From the demographic dividend of a young and rapidly expanding population to an increasingly affluent and aspirational middle class, Africa has the potential to become a new powerhouse of production and consumption in the 21st century, just as Asia was able to do in the late 20th. Over the past decade, consumer expenditure in the the league of East African Nations have grown at an average rate of 10.2% annually. This is testament to the demand potential and purchasing power of East Africans for digital and technology services. [Source: PwC, Disrupting East Africa, World Bank]
